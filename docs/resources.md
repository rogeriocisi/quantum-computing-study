## 📚 Free Books & Course Notes
*All resources are open and legal access.*

### 1. Introduction to Classical and Quantum Computing
**Thomas G. Wong · Rooted Grove, 2022 · 478 pages**
The best starting point. Requires only trigonometry — all linear algebra is developed from scratch. Covers qubits, gates, entanglement, Grover, Shor, and circuits.
*   [⬇ Download PDF](https://www.thomaswong.net/introduction-to-classical-and-quantum-computing-1e4p.pdf) | [🌐 Author Site](https://www.thomaswong.net/)
*   *Tags: Beginner, Linear Algebra, Algorithms*

### 2. Learn Quantum Computing using Qiskit
**IBM Qiskit Community · IBM Research · 500+ pages**
The official IBM book for learning quantum computing with real Python/Qiskit programming. Includes variational algorithms (VQE, QAOA).
*   [⬇ Download PDF](https://github.com/RafeyIqbalRahman/Qiskit-Textbook/raw/master/Learn%20Quantum%20Computing%20using%20Qiskit.pdf) | [📓 Jupyter Notebooks](https://github.com/Qiskit/textbook)
*   *Tags: Beginner–Intermediate, Qiskit, Python*

### 3. Quantum Computing for the Quantum Curious
**Hughes, Isaacson, Perry · Springer, 2021 · 158 pages**
Explains superposition and entanglement with minimal math. Ideal for a solid conceptual view.
*   [⬇ Download PDF](https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/978-3-030-61601-4.pdf)
*   *Tags: Beginner, Quantum Physics, Springer Open*

### 4. An Introduction to Quantum Computing
**Kaye, Laflamme & Mosca · Oxford University Press, 2007**
Classic text with rigorous mathematical foundations (Hilbert space, operators).
*   [⬇ Download PDF](https://files.batistalab.com/teaching/attachments/chem584/Mosca.pdf)
*   *Tags: Intermediate, Math Rigor, Algorithms*

### 5. Quantum Computing for Computer Scientists
**Yanofsky & Mannucci · Cambridge University Press, 2008**
Written specifically for computer scientists. Excellent coverage of computability and cryptography.
*   [⬇ Download PDF](https://quantumatlas.ir/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/Quantum-Computing-for-Computer-Scientists.pdf)
*   *Tags: Beginner–Intermediate, CS Focus*

### 6. Quantum Computing: A Gentle Introduction
**Eleanor Rieffel & Wolfgang Polak · MIT Press, 2011**
The essential bridge. Uses linear algebra instead of differential calculus. Highly recommended before Nielsen & Chuang.
*   [⬇ Download PDF](https://s3.amazonaws.com/arena-attachments/1000401/c8d3f8742d163b7ffd6ae3e4e4e07bf3.pdf)
*   *Tags: Essential Bridge, MIT Press, NASA QuAIL*

### 7. Quantum Computation and Quantum Information ("The Bible")
**Michael Nielsen & Isaac Chuang · Cambridge University Press**
The definitive and most prestigious text in the field. Reference for virtually every scientific paper.
*   [🌐 Official Page](https://www.cambridge.org/highereducation/books/quantum-computation-and-quantum-information/01E10196D0A682A6AEFFEA52D53BE9AE) | [🏛 Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/QuantumComputationAndQuantumInformation10thAnniversaryEdition)
*   *Tags: Advanced, Definitive Reference*

### 8. Quantum Computing: An Applied Approach
**Jack Hidary · Springer, 2021 (2nd ed.)**
Focuses on practical frameworks like Qiskit and Cirq. Covers VQE, QAOA, and QML.
*   [🌐 Springer Link](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-030-83274-2) | [💻 GitHub Code](https://github.com/jackhidary/quantumcomputingbook)
*   *Tags: Intermediate–Advanced, Qiskit + Cirq*

### 9. Dancing with Qubits
**Robert S. Sutor · Packt Publishing, 2019**
Visual and pedagogical introduction for IT professionals without a strong math background.
*   [🌐 View on Packt](https://www.packtpub.com/en-us/product/dancing-with-qubits-9781838827366)
*   *Tags: Beginner, IT Professional Focus*
